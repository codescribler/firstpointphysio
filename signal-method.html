01

The Problem With Most Business Websites

Most businesses struggle with their website copy. They know what they do — they're experts at it — but when it comes to writing it down in a way that resonates with potential customers, they're stuck. The result? Websites that talk about the business instead of talking to the customer.

This isn't their fault. Nobody teaches business owners how to write website copy. So they default to what they've seen — "Welcome to [business name]", a wall of text about their history and qualifications, and a contact page buried three clicks deep. It looks professional. It says nothing.

The consequence is simple: visitors leave. Not because the business isn't good at what it does, but because the website didn't give them a reason to stay. It didn't name their problem, didn't show them a clear path forward, and didn't make them feel understood. Confused people don't convert. They click away.

This is why we don't just design your website — we write the copy too. And we do it using a proven framework.

02

What Is The Signal Method?

The Signal Method is Dreamfree's approach to writing website copy that converts. It's built on the StoryBrand framework — a messaging methodology created by Donald Miller, used by thousands of businesses worldwide. The core insight is deceptively simple: if you confuse, you lose.

The principle is storytelling structure applied to marketing. Every great story has a hero with a problem who meets a guide, receives a plan, and is called to action — avoiding failure and ending in success. The same structure works on a website. The difference? Your customer is the hero. Your business is the guide. Most websites get this backwards — they make the business the hero of the story. That's why they don't convert.

"People don't buy the best products. They buy the products they can understand the fastest."

— Donald Miller, Building a StoryBrand

But The Signal Method isn't just "using StoryBrand." It's a complete process that turns your expertise into clear, compelling copy:

2

3

4

You don't need to write a single word. We handle it — because most businesses shouldn't be writing their own website copy. Not because they can't write, but because they're too close to their own business to see it the way a customer does.
