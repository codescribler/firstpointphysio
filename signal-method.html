01

The Problem With Most Business Websites

Most businesses struggle with their website copy. They know what they do — they're experts at it — but when it comes to writing it down in a way that resonates with potential customers, they're stuck. The result? Websites that talk about the business instead of talking to the customer.

This isn't their fault. Nobody teaches business owners how to write website copy. So they default to what they've seen — "Welcome to [business name]", a wall of text about their history and qualifications, and a contact page buried three clicks deep. It looks professional. It says nothing.

The consequence is simple: visitors leave. Not because the business isn't good at what it does, but because the website didn't give them a reason to stay. It didn't name their problem, didn't show them a clear path forward, and didn't make them feel understood. Confused people don't convert. They click away.

This is why we don't just design your website — we write the copy too. And we do it using a proven framework.

02

What Is The Signal Method?

The Signal Method is Dreamfree's approach to writing website copy that converts. It's built on the StoryBrand framework — a messaging methodology created by Donald Miller, used by thousands of businesses worldwide. The core insight is deceptively simple: if you confuse, you lose.

The principle is storytelling structure applied to marketing. Every great story has a hero with a problem who meets a guide, receives a plan, and is called to action — avoiding failure and ending in success. The same structure works on a website. The difference? Your customer is the hero. Your business is the guide. Most websites get this backwards — they make the business the hero of the story. That's why they don't convert.

"People don't buy the best products. They buy the products they can understand the fastest."

— Donald Miller, Building a StoryBrand

But The Signal Method isn't just "using StoryBrand." It's a complete process that turns your expertise into clear, compelling copy:

You don't need to write a single word. We handle it — because most businesses shouldn't be writing their own website copy. Not because they can't write, but because they're too close to their own business to see it the way a customer does.

03

The Seven-Part Story

Every effective website follows the same narrative structure. Here's how we applied each element of the StoryBrand framework to the First Point Physio website — and the specific copy decisions behind each one.

1
A Character
PCN Practice Managers and Clinical Directors who need a reliable FCP service

The hero of this website isn't First Point Physio — it's the practice manager responsible for commissioning FCP services within their Primary Care Network. Every headline, every section, every word is written for them.

2
Has a Problem
"Your GPs are spending a third of their day on MSK cases they shouldn't need to see"

The problem isn't just practical (GP overload, recruitment difficulty) — it's emotional. Practice managers feel stretched, anxious, and frustrated. The copy names this directly in the "Sound Familiar?" section, so visitors feel understood before they're asked to do anything.

3
Meets a Guide
First Point Physio — experienced, empathetic, and equipped

The website positions First Point Physio as the guide through two signals: empathy ("We Know the Pressure You're Under") and authority (Band 7+ FCPs, three regions, £30 vs £90 cost comparison, testimonials). Empathy without authority is weakness. Authority without empathy is arrogance. The site balances both.

4
Who Gives Them a Plan
Book a Discovery Call → We Handle Everything → Your GPs Focus on What Matters

When people see a clear path, they take it. The 3-step plan reduces the perceived complexity of outsourcing an FCP from "daunting project" to "simple conversation." Each step is named to reassure: you talk, we work, you benefit.

5
And Calls Them to Action
"Book a Discovery Call" — visible, repeated, consistent

The direct CTA appears in the navigation bar, the hero section, after the 3-step plan, in the service section, and in the footer. A transitional CTA — "Download Our Free PCN Guide" — catches visitors who aren't ready to talk yet but are willing to exchange their email.

6
That Helps Them Avoid Failure
Wasted ARRS funding, GP burnout, months-long recruitment gaps

The "Sound Familiar?" section doesn't just name the current problem — it hints at what happens if nothing changes. ARRS funding goes unspent. GPs remain overloaded. Recruitment attempts drag on. The copy creates just enough tension to motivate action without resorting to fear-mongering.

7
And Ends in Success
"Your GPs do what they trained for. Your patients get faster, better MSK care. Your ARRS funding works."

The "What Changes When You Work With Us" section paints the picture of life after: GP time reclaimed, ARRS budget utilised, zero operational burden, better patient outcomes. The visitor can see themselves in the success before they've even picked up the phone.

04

How This Shapes the Website Copy

Every decision on the website — from headline wording to button placement — maps back to a Signal Method principle. Here's exactly what we did and why.

Signal Method Principle What We Did on the Site
Customer is the hero The headline reads "Free Up Your GPs. We Handle MSK." — it's about what they get, not what we do. The word "your" appears before "we" in every key section.
Name the problem The "Sound Familiar?" section opens with: "Your GPs are spending a third of their day on musculoskeletal cases they shouldn't need to see." It names their exact daily reality.
Show empathy + authority "We Know the Pressure You're Under" (empathy) paired with a stats bar — Band 7+ FCPs, 3 regions, £30 vs £90 (authority). The tone says "we get it" while the numbers say "we can do it."
Give them a plan A 3-step process: "Book a Discovery Call → We Handle Everything → Your GPs Focus on What Matters." Reduces a complex outsourcing decision to three simple steps.
Repeat the CTA "Book a Discovery Call" appears in the navigation bar, hero section, after the 3-step plan, in the service overview, and in the footer — five times on one page.
Remove friction "One simple price — no hidden fees." "Flexible contract terms — we earn your trust, not lock you in." The service section includes a full checklist of what's included, eliminating ambiguity.
Paint success "What Changes When You Work With Us" — four benefit cards showing life after: GP time reclaimed, ARRS funding working, zero burden, better outcomes. The visitor sees the result before they've acted.
Transparent pricing "Each FCP appointment costs roughly £30 vs £90+ for an equivalent GP consultation." Stating cost early and clearly removes the biggest unspoken objection: "can we afford this?"
05

Why This Helps Practice Managers Book a Discovery Call

The Signal Method isn't just a writing technique — it's applied psychology. Here are the four principles that make it work, followed by a walk-through of exactly what a visitor experiences on the homepage.

The Four Principles

Clarity in 5 Seconds

The "Grunt Test" — could a caveman understand what you offer, how it helps, and what to do next within 5 seconds of landing? The hero section passes this test: managed FCPs, free up your GPs, book a discovery call.

Reduces Mental Effort

Confused visitors leave. Story structure gives the brain a familiar pattern to follow — problem, guide, plan, action — so visitors stay engaged without having to work out what to do next.

Answers the Real Questions

Practice managers aren't just asking "what do you do?" They're asking "will this actually work for us?" and "will it create more problems?" The copy answers the emotional questions, not just the practical ones.

Always a Clear Next Step

No dead ends. Every section on the homepage flows into the next, and every section ends with a path forward. The visitor is never left wondering "OK, now what?"

Practice managers don't book a discovery call because of features or qualifications. They book because the website made them feel confident — confident that their MSK problem will be handled competently, that the transition will be smooth, and that there won't be surprises.
