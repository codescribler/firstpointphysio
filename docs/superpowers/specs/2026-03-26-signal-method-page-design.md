# Signal Method Page — First Point Physio

## Overview

A standalone HTML page (`signal-method.html`) that explains the messaging strategy behind the First Point Physio website copy. It educates the client on why StoryBrand-based copy converts, differentiates Dreamfree's process (The Signal Method), and closes with a CTA.

## Technical Decisions

- **Single self-contained HTML file** — no external CSS/JS dependencies beyond Google Fonts
- **Design system:** Matches `proposal.html` — Figtree (headings) + Noto Sans (body), teal `#0B7285` primary, orange `#E8590C` CTA, light backgrounds
- **Components reused from proposal.html:** sticky header, numbered section circles, highlight boxes, feature card grids, tables, blockquotes, before/after comparison cards, CTA box, grain overlay, scroll-reveal animations, print styles
- **Meta:** `noindex, nofollow`, Microsoft Clarity analytics, responsive/mobile-first
- **Not linked** from `index.html` — standalone for now

### Section 3: The Seven-Part Story
Seven numbered steps with content from the BrandScript:

1. **A Character** — PCN Practice Managers and Clinical Directors needing FCP services
2. **Has a Problem** — GPs overwhelmed with MSK, recruitment is a nightmare, ARRS funding unused
3. **Meets a Guide** — First Point Physio understands the pressure, Band 7+ FCPs, multi-region coverage
4. **Who Gives Them a Plan** — Book a Discovery Call → We Handle Everything → Your GPs Focus on What Matters
5. **And Calls Them to Action** — "Book a Discovery Call" prominently placed throughout
6. **That Helps Them Avoid Failure** — Wasted ARRS funding, GP burnout, months-long recruitment gaps
7. **And Ends in Success** — GPs freed up, patients seen faster, zero operational burden

### Section 6: Generic Copy vs Signal Method Copy
Two side-by-side cards:

**Left — "Typical FCP Provider Website" (grey, X marks):**
- Leads with "Welcome to [Company Name]"
- Opens with founding story and company history
- Lists services without patient context
- One small contact link buried in navigation
- Visitor has to work out if it suits their PCN
- No clear path from interest to enquiry

**Right — "The Signal Method" (green, checkmarks):**
- Leads with what the practice manager wants
- Opens with their daily frustration
- Benefits framed around their operational reality
- CTA visible immediately and repeated throughout
- Site answers their objections proactively
- Guided journey from problem to discovery call

Closing: "Both websites describe the same service. The difference is that the Signal Method version is structured to help someone *decide*, not just *browse*."
