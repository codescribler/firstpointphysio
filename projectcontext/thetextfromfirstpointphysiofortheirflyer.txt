What we do
We see patients with suspected musculoskeletal (MSK) conditions, rather than them seeing a GP,  working alongside the primary care team with the aim to reduce pressure on GP’s . 
A first contact physiotherapist (FCP) is a fully qualified, experienced physiotherapist who is able to assess, diagnose and manage patients with a range of MSK conditions and screen for non-MSK conditions. They have undertaken enhanced and advanced training and have to follow the highest clinical and professional standards. They are also registered with the Health and Care Professions Council.
Our impact
MSK health issues are the most common cause of repeat GP appointments and research has shown that up to 30% of a GPs caseload at any one time is comprised of musculoskeletal complaints. Most of them can be dealt with effectively by a physiotherapist without any need to see the GP​.
MSK is the second largest cause of sickness absence; speeding up access to a physio will reduce this.

How does it work?
Patient contacts their GP Surgery regarding an issue.
Care navigator or triage clinician determines it is a MSK issue.
Care navigator books patient into the FCP diary on the surgery’s patient system.
FCP assesses and triages the  patient for red flags/serious pathology and ensures the condition is MSK in origin. We keep our notes within the GP surgery’s patient system.
Patient is then either 
given advice and exercises where appropriate and encouraged to self manage.
referred to Physiotherapy, further investigations, secondary care or other specialist services.
Exercises are given to patients through PhysioTec. We are also working in partnership with DocHQ to provide AI  to help patients perform exercises accurately at home.
Follow up appointments are available where necessary and depending on the needs of the practice.
