# First Point Physio — BrandScript 2

> **Purpose:** Revised BrandScript incorporating the client's own brochure, brand colours, messaging, and data. This version reflects how First Point Physio presents itself — a small, agile, personalised provider — while applying StoryBrand structure to sharpen the message for conversion.

> **Key differences from BrandScript 1:** Updated authority stats (50+ clinicians, 120+ surgeries, since 2017), client's own brand colour palette (navy/blue), their emphasis on being small and agile vs large corporate providers, real performance data from monthly reports, and contact details matching their brochure.

---

## PART 1: THE BRANDSCRIPT

---

### 1. CHARACTER (The Customer)

**Primary audience:** PCN Practice Managers and Clinical Directors responsible for commissioning First Contact Practitioner (FCP) services within their Primary Care Network.

**Secondary audience:** Patients who arrive at the site looking for information about their FCP appointment or exercise handouts.

**What the hero wants (one thing):**
A reliable, fully managed FCP service that takes MSK patients off their GP lists — without creating more admin, more recruitment headaches, or more risk for the practice.

---

### 2. THE PROBLEM

**External Problem:**
GP appointments are overwhelmed with musculoskeletal patients who don't need a GP — up to 30% of a GP's caseload at any one time. MSK is also the second largest cause of sickness absence, so faster access to a physiotherapist reduces this too. The practice doesn't have the clinical resource or time to set up and manage an FCP service in-house.

**Internal Problem:**
Practice managers feel stretched, anxious, and frustrated. They know ARRS funding is available but worry about the complexity of recruiting, supervising, and retaining qualified FCPs. They've seen large providers promise the world and deliver inconsistency. They're tired of juggling locums, covering sickness, chasing HR issues, and dealing with yet another operational headache they didn't train for. They want a partner they can actually talk to — not a faceless corporate machine.

**Philosophical Problem:**
GPs shouldn't be spending their time on MSK consultations that a specialist physiotherapist can handle better and faster. Patients deserve quicker access to the right clinician. Practices shouldn't have to choose between patient care quality and operational sanity. And a small, dedicated provider who knows your practice by name should be able to outperform a large one that treats you as a contract number.

**The Villain:**
The recruitment crisis, bureaucratic complexity, corporate provider inconsistency, and the hidden costs of trying to do it all in-house — wasted ARRS funding, unfilled roles, inconsistent cover, revolving-door locums, and the constant risk of staff turnover.

---

"We know how hard it is to recruit and retain qualified FCPs. We understand the pressure you're under to fill ARRS roles, keep appointments flowing, and maintain clinical standards — all while managing a dozen other priorities. You shouldn't have to become an MSK recruitment agency on top of everything else. And you shouldn't have to settle for a provider who doesn't know your practice or your team."

The director answers his phone. The team knows your practice by name. Issues get resolved the same day, not escalated through three tiers of account management. This is the core differentiator — and the website needs to lean into it hard.

### 6. FAILURE (What happens if they don't act)

- ARRS funding goes unspent or is clawed back — money that should be improving patient access is wasted
- GPs remain bogged down with MSK appointments — up to 30% of their caseload — leading to longer wait times across the board
- In-house recruitment attempts drag on for months, leaving gaps in service and frustrated patients
- Large corporate providers deliver inconsistency — rotating clinicians, impersonal account management, poor communication
- Without proper clinical governance, patient safety risk increases
- Acute MSK conditions become chronic because patients can't access timely specialist care
- Every week without an FCP, the practice loses the equivalent of dozens of GP appointments to conditions a physiotherapist could manage more effectively

## PART 7: COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

**Key differentiator vs large providers:**
First Point Physio should not try to compete on scale. The positioning should be: "We're not the biggest. We're the most attentive." The website should make the visitor feel like they'll be working with real people who care about their practice — not a corporate machine that sees them as a revenue line.

**Messaging pillars:**
1. **Small and agile** — we know your practice by name
2. **Longest-running** — since 2017, one of the earliest FCP providers
3. **Data-driven** — monthly bespoke reporting, not vague promises
4. **Fully managed** — recruitment, supervision, cover, CPD, governance
5. **Genuinely responsive** — the director answers his phone

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't use corporate language ("solutions", "synergies", "stakeholder engagement")
- Don't hide behind stock imagery — use real team photos where possible
- Don't bury the stats — 87%, 98%, 94% are powerful and should be prominent
- Don't overcomplicate the service description — it's simple: we put a physio in your surgery and manage everything
