> **Purpose:** This document contains the complete BrandScript, One-Liner, Grunt Test validation, and section-by-section homepage copy for First Point Physio. It is structured so that any AI tool or web developer can build the website directly from this brief without further interpretation.

## PART 1: THE BRANDSCRIPT

---

### 1. CHARACTER (The Customer)

**Primary audience:** PCN Practice Managers and Clinical Directors responsible for commissioning First Contact Practitioner (FCP) services within their Primary Care Network.

**Secondary audience:** Patients who arrive at the site looking for exercise handouts or information about their FCP appointment.

**What the hero wants (one thing):**
A reliable, fully managed FCP service that takes MSK patients off their GP lists — without creating more admin or risk for the practice.

---

### 2. THE PROBLEM

**External Problem:**
GP appointments are overwhelmed with musculoskeletal patients who don't need a GP — but the practice doesn't have the clinical resource or time to set up and manage an FCP service in-house.

**Internal Problem:**
Practice managers feel stretched, anxious, and frustrated. They know ARRS funding is available but worry about the complexity of recruiting, supervising, and retaining qualified FCPs. They're tired of juggling locums, covering sickness, and dealing with yet another operational headache they didn't train for.

**Philosophical Problem:**
GPs shouldn't be spending their time on MSK consultations that a specialist physiotherapist can handle better and faster. Patients deserve quicker access to the right clinician. Practices shouldn't have to choose between patient care quality and operational sanity.

**The Villain:**
The recruitment crisis, bureaucratic complexity, and the hidden costs of trying to do it all in-house — wasted ARRS funding, unfilled roles, inconsistent cover, and the constant risk of staff turnover.

---

### 3. THE GUIDE (First Point Physio)

**Empathy:**
"We know how hard it is to recruit and retain qualified FCPs. We understand the pressure you're under to fill ARRS roles, keep appointments flowing, and maintain clinical standards — all while managing a dozen other priorities. You shouldn't have to become an MSK recruitment agency on top of everything else."

**Authority:**
- Experienced team of Band 7+ First Contact Practitioners with advanced MSK qualifications
- Currently serving PCNs across the Midlands, South East, and Southern England
- Fully managed service: recruitment, supervision, CPD, holiday cover, and sickness cover all included
- Each FCP appointment costs roughly £30 vs £90+ for an equivalent GP consultation
- Existing case study demonstrating measurable GP workload reduction
- Exercise handout library already used by thousands of patients (the most-visited section of the current site — proof of clinical value)
- Testimonials from current PCN partners

### 6. FAILURE (What happens if they don't act)

- ARRS funding goes unspent or is clawed back — money that should be improving patient access is wasted
- GPs remain bogged down with MSK appointments they're overqualified to handle, leading to longer wait times across the board
- In-house recruitment attempts drag on for months, leaving gaps in service and frustrated patients
- Without proper clinical governance, patient safety risk increases
- Every week without an FCP, the practice loses the equivalent of dozens of GP appointments to conditions a physiotherapist could manage more effectively

---

**Before → After:**

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| GPs spending 20–30% of appointments on MSK cases | GPs freed up to focus on complex, high-need patients |

| Patients waiting weeks for an MSK appointment | Patients seen quickly by the right clinician, first time |
| Anxiety about clinical governance and quality | Full supervision, training, and reporting handled |
| Feeling like you're doing it all alone | A trusted partner who understands primary care |

**Success statement:**
"Your GPs do what they trained for. Your patients get faster, better MSK care. Your ARRS funding works. And you didn't have to recruit a single person."

## PART 5: DESIGN & IMAGERY DIRECTION

### Colour & Tone
- Professional, clean, and warm — this is healthcare, not a tech startup
- Primary palette should feel trustworthy (blues, greens, or teals work well) with a bold accent colour for CTA buttons (a warm orange, green, or contrasting colour that stands out clearly)
- Avoid clinical white — the site should feel approachable, not sterile

### Imagery Guidelines
- Hero image: physiotherapist with patient in a GP surgery / consultation room setting
- Supporting images: real practice environments, team photos in clinical settings, patients looking positive and active
- Avoid: stock photos of spines/skeletons, gym/sports settings, overly corporate imagery, images of people in severe pain
- For the patient-facing sections (exercise handouts, "what to expect"): warm, encouraging imagery of people doing everyday activities — walking, gardening, playing with children

### Typography
- Bold, highly readable heading font (avoid anything decorative)
- Clean sans-serif body font at 16px minimum
- Maximum 2 fonts throughout

### Navigation
- Header nav: Services | For PCNs | Results | About | Contact + **Book a Discovery Call** button (top right)
- Keep it to 5 items maximum + the CTA button
- All secondary pages live in the footer

### Mobile
- All sections stack cleanly
- CTA buttons minimum 44px tall, full-width on mobile
- Phone number tap-to-call
- Hero text readable without relying on background image overlay
